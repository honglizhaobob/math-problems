December 9, 2025 · 1 min
Question

Consider the finite-horizon LQR problem on $[0,T]$: $$ \dot{x}(t) = A x(t) + B u(t), \qquad x(0) = x_0. $$

The cost functional is $$ J(u) = \int_0^T \bigl( x(t)^\top Q x(t) + u(t)^\top R u(t) \bigr),dt + x(T)^\top S x(T). $$

  1. Show that the optimal control has the feedback form $$ u^\star(t) = -R^{-1} B^\top P(t) x^\star(t), $$ where $P(t)$ solves the Riccati equation with $P(T) = S$.
Solution
Sketch. Write down the Hamiltonian, derive the adjoint equation and optimality condition, and identify the Riccati equation for $P(t)$ so that $u^\star(t) = -R^{-1}B^\top P(t)x^\star(t)$.
