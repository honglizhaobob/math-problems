Turnpike Phenomenon in Finite-Horizon LQR

Topic: Optimal Control · Difficulty: ★★★☆

Problem

Consider the finite-horizon linear–quadratic control problem on $[0,T]$:

$$ \dot{x}(t) = A x(t) + B u(t), \qquad x(0) = x_0, $$

with cost functional

$$ J(u) = \int_0^T \bigl( x(t)^\top Q x(t) + u(t)^\top R u(t) \bigr)\,dt + x(T)^\top S x(T), $$

where $Q, R, S$ are symmetric positive definite matrices and $(A,B)$ is stabilizable.

  1. (Riccati equation) Show that the optimal control has the feedback form $$ u^\star(t) = - R^{-1} B^\top P(t) x^\star(t), $$ where $P(t)$ solves the Riccati differential equation $$ -\dot P(t) = A^\top P(t) + P(t) A - P(t) B R^{-1} B^\top P(t) + Q, \qquad P(T) = S. $$
  2. (Steady-state limit) Let $P_\infty$ denote the unique positive definite solution of the algebraic Riccati equation $$ 0 = A^\top P_\infty + P_\infty A - P_\infty B R^{-1} B^\top P_\infty + Q. $$ Argue (formally is fine) that when $T$ is large, $P(t)$ stays close to $P_\infty$ for most $t \in [0,T]$, except near the two endpoints.
  3. (Turnpike behavior) Consider the closed-loop system $$ \dot{x}^\star(t) = \bigl(A - B R^{-1} B^\top P(t)\bigr) x^\star(t). $$ Assuming exponential stability of $A - B R^{-1} B^\top P_\infty$, explain why the optimal trajectory $x^\star(t)$ spends most of its time near the steady-state $x = 0$, independently of the choice of $x_0$ and $T$, except for boundary layers near $t = 0$ and $t = T$.

Remarks

  • This is a prototype of the turnpike phenomenon: for long horizons, optimal trajectories are attracted to a time-independent steady state.
  • You can optionally try to make the above statements precise using Grönwall-type estimates for the Riccati equation and the closed-loop dynamics.
